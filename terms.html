VeriTrade Data Use and Processing Notice

This page sets out, in clear and formal terms, what data VeriTrade collects, how that data is processed, and the limits that apply to identity comparison information used by the service.

1. Data We Collect

VeriTrade may collect and process the following information when a user registers, signs in, creates a seller profile, or records a seller assessment as a buyer:

  • Account registration information, including username, full name, and account email address.
  • Contact information, including phone number where submitted by the user.
  • Seller profile information, including marketplace selections, social handle, and physical address details entered by the seller.
  • Buyer-created seller assessment information, including seller name, seller email address, seller phone number, and the final safety score.
  • Transaction follow-up information, including delivery outcome updates and fraud report status.
  • Service activity records, including verification events, history entries, and analytics summaries required for workspace functionality and service administration.

2. Data We Do Not Store

VeriTrade does not retain uploaded identity documents or identity photographs as permanent stored records within the buyer-created seller assessment workflow.

  • Seller ID numbers entered on the buyer page are not saved to the database.
  • Seller ID images uploaded on the buyer page are not saved to the database.
  • Seller selfie or portrait images uploaded on the buyer page are not saved to the database.
  • These items are used only for comparison verification checks within the platform's identity assessment flow.

3. How We Process the Data

VeriTrade processes user data solely for the operation of the platform's verification, record-keeping, and post-purchase management functions.

  • Registration and sign-in data are processed to create and maintain a user account and to control access to the platform.
  • Seller profile data are processed to support seller registration status, trust-related display features, and marketplace visibility within the service.
  • Buyer-recorded seller assessment data are processed to generate a safety record and to support later post-purchase actions on the user page.
  • Status updates and fraud reports are processed to preserve historical warning signals relevant to future checks and account activity history.
  • History and analytics data are processed to provide account summaries, verification histories, and workspace insights.

4. Identity Comparison Verification

The identity verification component of VeriTrade performs a comparison check between an uploaded identity image and an uploaded selfie image for the purpose of generating a verification outcome within the platform workflow.

Those materials are used only for comparison verification checks and are not stored as a permanent identity archive in the buyer-created seller record.

5. Buyer-Saved Seller Records

When a buyer saves a seller assessment, VeriTrade stores only the fields required for later reference and post-purchase management.

  • Seller name.
  • Seller email address.
  • Seller phone number.
  • Final safety score.
  • Post-purchase workflow fields, including delivery status and fraud report status.

6. User Responsibility

Users are responsible for ensuring that information submitted to VeriTrade is accurate, relevant to the intended verification purpose, and lawful to share. VeriTrade is designed to support trust assessment and transaction follow-up and should be used accordingly.
